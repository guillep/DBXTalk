Overview

I'm useful when classes needs to be created during the execution of the test. This avoid polluting your unit tests with dummy and mock classes. A typical usage of it is:

TestCase subclass: #YourTest instanceVariableNames: 'classFactory'

YourTest>>setUp classFactory := ClassFactoryForTestCase new

YourTest>>tearDown classFactory deleteClasses.

YourTest>>testIsBehavior | cls | cls := classFactory newClass. self assert: cls isBehavior
